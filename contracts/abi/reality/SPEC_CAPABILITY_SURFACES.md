# Dominium Capability Surfaces v1

## A. Purpose And Scope

This document defines the constitutional capability-surface framework for Dominium after `Λ-0` and `Λ-1`.

It exists so later systems can answer, in one lawful model:

- what can be done
- by whom or what
- under what conditions
- through which declared interaction surfaces
- without confusing truth, observation, affordance, and UI

This framework governs:

- the meaning of a capability surface
- the distinction between capability surfaces and nearby concepts
- the high-level taxonomy of lawful capability-surface classes
- the actor classes that may use those surfaces
- the validity, visibility, composition, failure, and verification expectations for capability surfaces

This framework does not yet define:

- every domain-specific capability instance
- concrete gameplay interactions
- concrete control bindings
- runtime input or service implementations
- cross-domain ownership winners reserved for later review

This framework inherits from `Λ-0` and `Λ-1`.
It does not compete with them.
Later domain constitutions, governance prompts, and runtime prompts must use this document as the interaction and action floor.

## B. Core Definition

A capability surface is a lawful, machine-readable interaction surface exposed over domain-grounded substrate and affordances.

It describes:

- the action families that may be attempted
- the actor classes that may attempt them
- the conditions that make the surface valid, detectable, usable, or authorized
- the lawful Process paths the surface routes into
- the refusal, degradation, or failure boundaries that apply

A capability surface is distinct from the following:

- `primitive truth`:
  the truth-bearing substrate itself
- `recognition`:
  a recognized pattern or interpreted structure over substrate
- `affordance`:
  a domain-level actionable relationship
- `observation channel`:
  a way to inspect or perceive state
- `UI or control binding`:
  a presentation or input mapping onto a surface

A capability surface is needed because the project must support embodied play, planning, AI, automation, institutional action, and future interfaces without redefining interaction rules separately for each interface.

## C. Relationship To Truth

Capability surfaces do not create truth.

They expose lawful action structure over truth-bearing substrate that already exists under domain contracts.

Capability surfaces therefore:

- must be grounded in domain contracts and host structures
- must not silently redefine ontology
- must not create new powers by presentation convenience
- must not turn absent domain structure into fake interaction magic

Surface invocation is not truth mutation.
Truth mutation still occurs only through lawful Processes under Law and AuthorityContext.

Resolved ambiguity:

- capability surfaces cannot redefine truth
- capability surfaces cannot bypass lawful Processes

## D. Relationship To Affordances

Affordances are domain-level actionable relationships.
They express that some actor, Assembly, system, or institution may attempt some class of action under lawful conditions.

Capability surfaces operate one layer above that:

- they provide a machine-readable and interaction-oriented exposure of one or more affordances
- they state how those affordances are made actionable for specific actor classes
- they preserve the domain meaning beneath them

Capability surfaces may:

- expose a single affordance directly
- aggregate multiple related affordances into one composite surface
- adapt one lawful affordance family for multiple access modes

Capability surfaces must not:

- erase which domain affordances they depend on
- invent capability meaning with no host affordance structure
- flatten all domain-specific affordances into one universal verb soup

## E. Relationship To Recognitions

Recognized structures may host capability surfaces.

For example:

- a recognized institution may host institutional action surfaces
- a recognized route or network node may host transport or access surfaces
- a recognized machine state may host maintenance or control surfaces

Capability surfaces must not replace recognitions or primitive truth.

They must preserve the chain:

- primitive truth remains substrate
- recognitions remain recognized meaning
- affordances remain domain action relationships
- capability surfaces remain exposed action surfaces

Interaction surfaces over recognized structures are lawful only when the recognized structure remains traceable to the underlying substrate and domain contract.

## F. Capability Surface Classes

The following high-level capability-surface classes are constitutionally recognized:

- `embodied_manipulation`:
  direct embodied interaction with objects, bodies, materials, tools, or environmental features
- `occupancy_use_point`:
  position-bound, slot-bound, seating, docking, standing, mounting, entry, or use-point access surfaces
- `transport_control`:
  steering, routing, piloting, dispatch, loading, or motion-control surfaces over vehicles, routes, or transport systems
- `machine_process_io`:
  machine, instrument, or process input/output/control surfaces over law-governed process hosts
- `route_network_access`:
  access, connection, linking, switching, or transit surfaces over route, graph, signal, or network structures
- `institutional_organizational`:
  policy, authority, jurisdiction, assignment, certification, governance, or coordination surfaces over institutional structures
- `planning_command`:
  abstract planning, scheduling, commanding, requesting, reserving, or delegation surfaces over lawful substrate
- `inspection_instrumentation`:
  inspect, measure, probe, diagnose, or audit surfaces that expose lawful observation or inspection actions
- `maintenance_repair`:
  service, repair, calibration, restoration, reconfiguration, or upkeep surfaces
- `automation_integration`:
  machine-readable orchestration, integration, protocol, or automation access surfaces
- `interface_adapter`:
  interface translation surfaces that map lawful capabilities into specific interface modes without redefining meaning

Domains may extend this taxonomy later, but only by specialization.
They may not locally erase or contradict these class meanings.

## G. Actor Classes

Capability surfaces may be exposed to different actor classes.
At the constitutional level the recognized actor classes are:

- `embodied_player`
- `embodied_npc`
- `abstract_planner`
- `ai_agent`
- `organization_institution`
- `machine_automation`
- `remote_operator`
- `future_interface_adapter`

Actor class support is always explicit.
No capability surface is assumed to be universally available to all actor classes.

Actor class support may vary by:

- AuthorityContext
- LawProfile
- epistemic scope
- proximity or embodiment
- host state
- institutional authority
- connection or integration state

## H. Preconditions And Constraints

Capability surfaces must define or reference the conditions under which they are valid.

These conditions may include:

- proximity
- occupancy or attachment
- compatibility
- access rights or entitlements
- knowledge or instrumentation
- energy, material, or signal connection
- organizational authority
- host state, health, or availability
- safety state
- declared scheduling or timing windows

Capability surfaces must not be assumed always-on.
They may exist while being:

- undetectable
- unusable
- unauthorized
- blocked
- degraded

Conditions must be explicit enough that later systems can refuse or degrade lawfully rather than guessing.

## I. Results And Effects

A capability surface may authorize or initiate:

- an inspection request
- an intent submission
- a plan or command request
- a reservation or routing request
- a maintenance or repair request
- a configuration or integration request

Those results are not truth mutation by surface magic.

The capability surface may:

- expose that an action path exists
- package the attempt into a lawful invocation envelope
- route the attempt to the appropriate Process family
- declare refusal and degradation boundaries

Truth mutation still happens only when the invoked Process is lawfully admitted and committed.

## J. Embodied Versus Abstract Access

The same underlying substrate may be accessed through embodied and abstract paths.

Examples of lawful variation include:

- embodied actors require proximity or physical access
- planners operate over abstract command or reservation surfaces
- AI agents act through explicit machine-readable surfaces
- institutions act through organizational authority surfaces

All of those must remain lawful projections of the same reality.

Abstract access must not:

- grant impossible powers
- ignore embodiment or access limits when those limits matter
- bypass law, capability, or causality
- invent actions that the substrate and domain contracts do not support

Resolved ambiguity:

- abstract access cannot grant impossible powers

## K. AI, Automation, And Future Interfaces

Capability surfaces are part of the project's interface-agnostic reality model.

They must support:

- machine-readable planning
- AI agents
- automation workflows
- remote operation
- future interface adapters

Future interfaces must map onto lawful capability surfaces rather than redefining ontology or creating UI-only power.

This keeps:

- player interaction
- AI action
- automation
- institutional workflows
- tool-driven operation

inside one shared law-governed action model.

## L. Visibility And Discoverability

A capability surface may exist even if a given actor cannot currently perceive or use it.

The following distinctions are mandatory:

- `existence`:
  the surface is lawful and attached to substrate
- `detectability`:
  the actor can discover or perceive it
- `usability`:
  the actor can practically invoke it under current conditions
- `authorization`:
  the actor is lawfully permitted to attempt it

These are not the same thing.

Observation and discoverability are shaped by epistemic and diegetic refinement, but they do not create or destroy capability surfaces silently.

## M. Composition And Hierarchy

Complex systems may expose composite capability surfaces.

For example:

- a machine may expose control, inspection, maintenance, and automation surfaces
- an institution may expose command, authorization, and audit surfaces
- a transport network may expose route access, control, and monitoring surfaces

Composite surfaces are lawful only when they preserve the structure beneath them.

Composition must therefore remain explicit about:

- child or host surfaces
- aggregated affordances
- preserved invariants
- expansion or escalation triggers

Opaque monolith surfaces that erase underlying meaning are forbidden.

## N. Failure And Invalidity

Capability surfaces may be:

- `available`
- `blocked`
- `degraded`
- `unavailable`
- `unsafe`
- `invalid`

Failure and invalidity conditions must be explicit.

Examples include:

- missing authority
- missing host connection
- invalid state
- degraded instrumentation
- failed maintenance condition
- unsafe environment
- incompatible actor or interface
- stale or incomplete recognition

A capability surface never implies guaranteed success.
It exposes lawful attempt structure, not guaranteed outcome.

## O. Verification

Capability surfaces must be testable and auditable.

Later systems should be able to verify:

- actor compatibility
- precondition satisfaction
- authority and epistemic gating
- lawful Process routing
- refusal and degradation determinism
- consistency with the host domain contract
- preservation of capability meaning across interface adapters

Verification may include:

- schema validation
- static checks
- contract review
- replay or audit evidence
- cross-checks against affordance and Process references

## P. Anti-Patterns And Forbidden Shapes

The following shapes are forbidden:

- UI-only pseudo-surfaces with no lawful substrate meaning
- hardcoded object tags standing in for real capability
- surfaces that directly mutate truth without Processes
- surfaces that collapse observation into access or authorization
- surfaces that silently grant abilities absent from lawful substrate
- domain-specific hacks pretending to be universal interaction law
- adapter surfaces that redefine ontology instead of mapping onto lawful surfaces
- convenience surfaces that hide refusal, degradation, or authority boundaries

Resolved ambiguity:

- UI bindings are not capability surfaces
- capability surfaces are not control bindings
- capability meaning may not be inferred from ad hoc local tags alone

## Q. Stability And Evolution

Stability class: `stable`.

This artifact is stable enough for later `Λ`, `Σ`, and `Φ` prompts to cite directly.

Later prompts may:

- instantiate domain-specific capability surfaces
- specialize capability classes
- refine precondition and verification structures
- add bridge and representation details

Later prompts may not:

- silently override the distinction between truth, affordance, capability surface, observation, and UI
- redefine capability surfaces as local runtime convenience structures
- authorize direct mutation by surface invocation
- collapse actor-class distinctions into presentation-only rules

Any future change that alters the meaning of capability surfaces requires:

- explicit artifact update
- explicit versioning
- explicit downstream review
- explicit semantic contract pinning where applicable
