# Live Cutover Receipt Pipeline Anchorization

## A. Purpose And Scope

This doctrine exists because the post-later-wave refresh checkpoint admitted exactly one later-wave bounded `Ζ` family:

- `zeta.family.live_cutover_receipt_pipeline_anchorization`

That admission is narrow and caution-gated.
It exists only in a shadow or parallel evidence posture and does not authorize live receipt-pipeline realization.

This doctrine solves a specific problem:

- later-wave receipt work now has one admitted lane, but that lane can drift into realization language unless its posture is frozen explicitly
- generalized live-cutover receipt law already exists, but the repo still needs one bounded doctrine for how anchorization may structure evidence without claiming live operational authority
- reclassification pressure is especially dangerous here because local traces, mirrors, CI summaries, and shadow evidence can look substantial while still being non-canonical

This doctrine governs:

- what receipt-pipeline anchorization means in this bounded later-wave context
- what shadow and parallel evidence posture mean for this family
- what anchorization classes and evidence classes are in scope
- what reclassification boundaries require `FULL` review
- how this family remains subordinate to operator transaction receipts, generalized live-cutover receipts, trust and revocation continuity, runtime doctrine, and distributed-authority boundaries
- what later checkpoints and later-wave planning must consume rather than reinvent

This doctrine does not govern:

- live receipt-pipeline implementation
- live cutover implementation
- trust, revocation, or publication execution
- distributed-runtime realization
- authority handoff or state transfer
- shard relocation

This is bounded later-wave evidence and anchorization doctrine, not live realization.

## B. Core Definition

In this bounded later-wave context, `receipt-pipeline anchorization` means the constitutional structuring, linkage, and anchoring of cutover-sensitive receipt evidence so that later review can assess reconstructability, lineage, refusal posture, and continuity without promoting that evidence into canonical live receipt-pipeline truth.

The key posture terms are:

- `shadow evidence posture`
  - evidence may be recorded, linked, or structured for review, but it does not become canonical live receipt truth by existence
- `parallel evidence posture`
  - evidence may sit alongside upstream operator receipts and generalized live-cutover receipt semantics as a companion anchor set, but it may not replace those upstream canonical surfaces

This family differs from nearby concepts:

- `live receipt-pipeline realization`
  - realization would claim that canonical live receipt flow is operational; anchorization does not
- `broader live cutover realization`
  - anchorization may preserve cutover-sensitive evidence posture, but it does not prove lawful live cutover
- `trust or publication execution realization`
  - trust-aware continuity may constrain anchorization, but no trust, revocation, or publication execution is realized here
- `ordinary logging or tracing`
  - logs and traces may supply inputs, but anchorization is a governed evidence-structuring doctrine, not a synonym for tool output

## C. Why This Family Is Admitted Only With Shadow/Parallel Evidence Posture

This family is admitted only with shadow or parallel evidence posture because the later-wave gate freeze admitted it only as `admitted_with_cautions`.

The repo truth behind that caution remains active:

- `data/registries/trust_root_registry.json` still exposes an empty trust-root set
- `data/registries/trust_policy_registry.json` remains provisional
- lawful runtime cutover proof remains unresolved
- canonical live receipt-pipeline realization remains unresolved
- trust, revocation, and publication execution remain unrealized

That means this family is allowed to reduce ambiguity, not to cross realization boundaries.

It may lawfully:

- structure shadow receipt lineage
- preserve parallel anchor packets for later review
- correlate operator-transaction, cutover-sensitive, and trust-aware evidence in bounded form
- record blocked, refused, partial, or shadow-only outcomes honestly

It may not lawfully:

- claim canonical live receipt-pipeline realization
- claim broader live cutover realization
- promote local evidence into canonical receipt truth without review
- create operational authority by evidence packaging alone

## D. Doctrine, Admission, Validation, And Realization Distinction

These four layers are distinct and must stay distinct:

- `doctrine`
  - defines what anchorization means and what it does not mean
- `admission`
  - allows this one family to proceed inside the later-wave bounded envelope under caution
- `validation`
  - allows bounded checking, structuring, refusal, and reconstructability assessment of evidence posture
- `realization`
  - would claim live receipt-pipeline, trust-bearing, or cutover-bearing operational maturity, which remains outside this family

The governing consequence is explicit:

- anchorization is not realization
- admission is not realization
- evidence structure is not operational authority
- shadow or parallel posture is not live canonical receipt flow

## E. Anchorization Classes

The following anchorization classes are in scope.

### E.1 Shadow Receipt Lineage Anchorization

Structures a companion lineage for cutover-sensitive receipt candidates while remaining explicitly shadow-only and non-promoting.

### E.2 Parallel Cutover-Window Anchorization

Structures a parallel evidence packet around a declared cutover-sensitive window without claiming that the window proves live cutover completion.

### E.3 Operator-Transaction Linkage Anchorization

Links candidate anchorization outputs back to upstream operator transaction receipts and continuity surfaces rather than inventing a parallel transaction canon.

### E.4 Proof-Packet Anchorization

Structures bounded proof-bearing inputs, refusal posture, and reconstructability anchors so later review can assess what was shown, what was missing, and what remained blocked.

### E.5 Trust-Aware Non-Executing Anchorization

Preserves trust-relevant or revocation-relevant continuity cues where they constrain receipt posture, while remaining strictly non-executing and non-promoting.

### E.6 Blocked Or Refused Anchorization

Preserves why a cutover-sensitive evidence path was blocked, refused, partial, or held for review instead of letting the record collapse into silence.

## F. Evidence Classes

The following evidence classes are in scope.

### F.1 Upstream Operator Receipt Evidence

Typed operator transaction receipts and continuity records that remain the baseline upstream evidence layer.

### F.2 Generalized Live-Cutover Receipt Evidence

Cutover-sensitive receipt semantics already frozen upstream, consumed here as governing law rather than redefined locally.

### F.3 Anchor Lineage Evidence

Evidence that preserves how shadow or parallel anchors link across intent, packet assembly, refusal, review, and bounded result posture.

### F.4 Cutover-Window Continuity Evidence

Evidence that records the bounded cutover-sensitive window, its continuity references, and its explicit non-realization posture.

### F.5 Runtime Boundary Reference Evidence

Evidence that records lifecycle, replay, snapshot, hotswap, or distributed-authority boundary relevance without claiming those runtime boundaries are already solved.

### F.6 Trust And Revocation Correlation Evidence

Evidence that preserves trust-relevant or revocation-relevant continuity linkage without executing trust change or publication change.

### F.7 Blocked Or Refused Evidence

Evidence that preserves why a candidate anchorization path did not become promotable or admissible.

### F.8 Derived Trace Evidence

Logs, traces, CI summaries, dashboards, mirror views, or bundle reports that may inform review but remain derived until explicit review promotes their meaning.

## G. Reclassification Boundary Rules

The review posture categories in this family are:

- `shadow_only`
  - evidence may be structured for review but not promoted
- `parallel_only`
  - evidence may accompany canonical receipts but not replace them
- `full_review_reclassification`
  - any promotion or semantic reclassification requires explicit `FULL` review
- `prohibited_live_authority_promotion`
  - some promotions are constitutionally outside this family and therefore forbidden

The following boundaries require `FULL` review:

### G.1 Derived-To-Canonical Receipt Promotion

Any attempt to promote local traces, logs, CI summaries, mirror views, or other derived evidence into canonical receipt meaning requires `FULL` review.

### G.2 Shadow Anchor To Live Pipeline Claim

Any attempt to reinterpret shadow or parallel anchorization as proof that a live receipt pipeline now exists requires `FULL` review and remains outside this family unless a later checkpoint explicitly admits it.

### G.3 Provenance Classification Or Event-Type Rebinding

Any attempt to reinterpret provenance classifications, provenance event semantics, or anchor classes in a way that changes canonical meaning requires `FULL` review.

### G.4 Trust, Revocation, Or Publication Semantic Promotion

Any attempt to let anchorization imply trust execution, revocation propagation realization, or publication execution requires `FULL` review and remains out of scope here.

### G.5 Runtime Or Distributed-Authority Semantic Promotion

Any attempt to let anchorization imply lawful cutover, authority handoff, state transfer, or distributed runtime convergence requires `FULL` review and remains out of scope here.

### G.6 Review Is Necessary But Not Always Sufficient

`FULL` review is mandatory for reclassification, but review does not by itself guarantee admission. Some promotions remain prohibited until later checkpoints explicitly narrow the blocker field.
