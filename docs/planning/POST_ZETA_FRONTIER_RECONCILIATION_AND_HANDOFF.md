## D. Frontier Classification

### D.1 Near-Term Research / Program Candidates

These are the first post-`Ζ` planning candidates because they are closest to already-frozen doctrine, but they still remain realization-absent:

- trust-aware refusal and containment as an execution-family question
- deterministic replication and state-partition transfer proof
- distributed replay and proof-anchor continuity realization
- lawful runtime cutover proof under hotswap and distributed-authority boundaries

These are still not admitted implementation work.
They are the most honest next research and planning layer because `Ζ` already narrowed their boundary conditions.

### D.2 Medium-Term Architecture Work

These require stronger architecture maturity and broader cross-domain convergence before they can leave frontier status:

- bounded authority handoff and state transfer
- distributed trust and authority convergence
- live trust, revocation, and publication execution
- live receipt-pipeline realization beyond shadow or parallel evidence posture

These remain downstream of the near-term frontier because they depend on stronger proof, compatibility, provenance, and authority law than the repo currently has.

### D.3 Long-Term Perimeter / Future-Only

These remain explicitly outside near-term execution:

- live shard relocation
- extreme pipe-dream live operations

They remain frozen as perimeter items rather than hidden backlog.

## E. Handoff Rules From Ζ To Post-Ζ

Post-`Ζ` planning must obey all of the following:

- do not reopen internal `Ζ` family execution under a new name
- preserve `Ζ-A0` as bounded-only
- preserve `Ζ-A1` and `Ζ-A2` as proof-only
- preserve `Ζ-B3` as guarded later-wave doctrine in shadow or parallel evidence posture only
- preserve `FULL` review for any evidence reclassification
- preserve the explicit distinction among doctrine completion, admission completion, and realization absence
- treat frontier items as candidate planning problems, not implicit admissions

## F. Post-Ζ Anti-Patterns

The following are forbidden:

- reopening `Ζ` implicitly by calling frontier realization work a continuation of `Ζ`
- treating frontier classification as admission
- treating doctrine completion as realization completion
- treating shadow or parallel receipt evidence as canonical live receipt truth
- treating runtime or trust code presence as proof that frontier blockers are solved
- using domain constitution drafting to smuggle live-ops realization back in through abstraction

## G. Handoff Outcome

The handoff outcome is explicit:

- admissible `Ζ` remains closed
- the frontier is explicit and classified
- the next program should freeze reusable post-`Ζ` templates and begin the domain constitution drafting run
- later post-`Ζ` checkpoints may reassess frontier maturity, but only outside internal `Ζ`
