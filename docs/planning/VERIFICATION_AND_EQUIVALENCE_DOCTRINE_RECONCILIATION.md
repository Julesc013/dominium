# Verification And Equivalence Doctrine Reconciliation

## A. Purpose And Scope

This artifact is `POST-ZETA-5`.

It reconciles one project-wide verification and equivalence layer across:

- semantic doctrine
- runtime doctrine
- release and provenance doctrine
- bounded `Ζ` validation, rehearsal, and anchorization doctrine

Its purpose is to stop future domain constitutions and frontier planning from using incompatible meanings of proof, equivalence, replay, or continuity.

## B. Unified Verification Dimensions

The unified dimensions are:

### B.1 Determinism

Verification must preserve:

- deterministic Process mutation
- deterministic ordering and reductions
- explicit compatibility identities for any non-identical but lawful forms

### B.2 Replay

Verification must preserve:

- event-log intelligibility
- lawful replay boundaries
- snapshot and replay continuity
- refusal when replay equivalence is not strong enough

### B.3 Substitution And Capsule Safety

Verification must preserve:

- declared preserved invariants
- lawful substitution boundaries
- expansion or refusal triggers
- boundedness against semantic leakage

### B.4 Macro ↔ Micro Equivalence

Verification must preserve:

- continuity between summary and detailed forms
- identity and provenance anchors
- no silent contradiction between macro commitments and micro expansion

### B.5 Experiential Continuity

Verification must preserve:

- lawful continuity for observers and players
- no unexplained retcons or discontinuities from optimization or scale changes

### B.6 Compatibility Over Time

Verification must preserve:

- explicit version and compatibility posture
- historical lineage
- refusal instead of silent reinterpretation

### B.7 Boundedness And Refusal

Verification must preserve:

- refusal when doctrine or evidence is insufficient
- bounded envelopes for proof-only, bounded-only, or shadow-only work

### B.8 Explainability And Evidence

Verification must preserve:

- evidence typing
- provenance lineage
- explainable reasoning from doctrine to result

## C. Evidence Classes

The canonical evidence classes are:

- `doctrinal_conformance_evidence`
- `schema_or_structure_evidence`
- `determinism_and_replay_evidence`
- `substitution_invariance_evidence`
- `macro_micro_equivalence_evidence`
- `continuity_and_provenance_evidence`
- `operator_receipt_and_anchorization_evidence`
- `refusal_or_block_evidence`
- `realization_evidence`

These classes may be specialized later.
They may not be collapsed into one vague notion of “proof”.

## D. Proof Distinctions

The project must keep these proof classes distinct:

### D.1 Proof Of Doctrine

Shows:

- the law, template, or boundary exists
- prose and machine-readable mirrors align
- the governing distinctions are explicit

### D.2 Proof Of Admission

Shows:

- a checkpoint or gate explicitly allowed bounded work
- the relevant guardrails and stop conditions exist

### D.3 Proof Of Realization

Shows:

- live or operational machinery actually satisfies the needed runtime, trust, provenance, or authority conditions

### D.4 Equivalence-Class Expectations

Shows:

- whether two forms are required to be exact, behaviorally equivalent, lineage-preserving, or only admissible with explicit refusal of stronger equivalence claims

## E. Equivalence Classes

The canonical equivalence classes are:

- `exact_identity_equivalence`
- `deterministic_replay_equivalence`
- `behavioral_equivalence_with_preserved_invariants`
- `macro_micro_lineage_preserving_equivalence`
- `substitution_safe_bounded_equivalence`
- `compatibility_preserving_non_identical_equivalence`
- `non_equivalent_but_explainably_refused`

## F. Relationship To Ζ Closure

`Ζ` closed several important verification boundaries:

- `Ζ-A0` proves bounded validation doctrine, not live transition realization
- `Ζ-A1` proves proof-only verification doctrine, not distributed replay realization
- `Ζ-A2` proves proof-only rehearsal doctrine, not live cutover realization
- `Ζ-B3` proves guarded anchorization doctrine, not live receipt-pipeline realization
- `Ζ-B4` proves trust-aware reconciliation doctrine, not execution-family admission

Those distinctions remain binding inputs to future verification language.

## G. Anti-Patterns

The following are forbidden:

- proof of doctrine presented as proof of realization
- admission evidence presented as runtime legality proof
- local replay success presented as universal equivalence
- shadow or parallel evidence presented as canonical receipt truth
- compatibility over time inferred from naming convenience
- macro summaries treated as lawful without micro re-entry obligations

## H. Reconciliation Outcome

The project-wide verification and equivalence layer is now frozen as a reusable post-`Ζ` planning floor.

Future domain constitutions must cite this layer when they define:

- what must be exact
- what may be behaviorally equivalent
- what remains only boundedly equivalent
- what must be refused rather than overclaimed
