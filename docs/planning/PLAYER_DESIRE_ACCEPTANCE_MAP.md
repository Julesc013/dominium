# Dominium Player Desire Acceptance Map v1

## A. Purpose And Scope

This document defines the canonical player-desire and actor-desire acceptance map for Dominium after `Λ-0` through `Λ-4`.

It exists because Dominium cannot be judged by abstract architecture elegance alone. It must be judged by the lawful desires its substrate can support across players, NPCs, AI agents, institutions, operators, and future interfaces.

This acceptance map:

- converts the semantic framework into explicit acceptance criteria
- maps desires to lawful substrate instead of magic feature promises
- gives later prompts and implementations a shared purpose-grounded constraint system
- identifies where support requires domain structure, capability surfaces, formalization stages, scaling rules, and refusal/failure classes
- records where the live repo and planning stack already embody strong grounding versus where later work is still required

This acceptance map is not:

- a marketing feature list
- a release roadmap
- a UX flow specification
- a completion claim
- a replacement for domain constitutions or runtime design

It inherits from `Λ-0` through `Λ-4`. It does not define every domain in detail, and it does not implement the gameplay or runtime systems that later prompts must build.

## B. Core Doctrine

Dominium architecture must be judged by what lawful desires it can support, refuse, degrade, or defer without semantic drift.

The governing rules are:

- desires must map to substrate, Process, Law, and Authority rather than wishful features
- acceptance criteria must remain compatible with Truth totality and sparse materialization
- capability surfaces expose lawful action but do not redefine truth
- representation ladders and semantic ascent/descent preserve invariants and lineage rather than license fake ontology
- formalization chain stages determine when designs, standards, and institutions become portable, reusable, and civilizationally scalable
- ordinary life is first-class, not decorative flavor
- engineering, standards, institutions, and civilization are first-class, not optional late extras
- AI agents, operators, and future interfaces are alternate lawful access modes over the same reality, not separate ontologies

Resolved ambiguity:

- the project cannot be judged by elegance alone
- ordinary life is not second-class
- engineering, industry, institutions, and civilization are not optional extras
- AI and future interfaces do not require a separate ontology

## C. Actor Classes

This map supports the following high-level actor classes:

- `embodied_player`: a player acting through embodied, local, diegetic, and law-governed pathways
- `abstract_planner_player`: a player acting through abstract planning, reservation, delegation, or command pathways
- `npc_labor_actor`: an embodied or delegated non-player actor performing labor, service, logistics, or institutional roles
- `ai_agent`: an AI actor using lawful machine-readable surfaces and governed authority
- `institution_organization`: a guild, school, bureaucracy, factory, polity, firm, or other organized actor acting through formal authority
- `operator_auditor`: an observer, inspector, moderator, administrator, analyst, or remote operator acting through lawful observation, audit, and control surfaces
- `future_interface_user`: a user or system acting through future adapters, remote projections, machine mediation, or new interface modes over the same substrate
- `machine_automation`: a machine or automation system operating within explicit protocol, authority, and verification limits

These classes correspond to and refine the actor model already grounded in `Λ-2`.

## G. Ordinary-Life Doctrine

Ordinary life is not a second-class mode.

The project must treat the following as first-class acceptance demands:

- habitation
- eating and provisioning
- routine work
- maintenance and upkeep
- local travel
- education and apprenticeship
- family, membership, and household continuity
- neighborhood and settlement belonging
- local trade and routine exchange
- ordinary institutional participation

If Dominium only supports extraordinary moments while ordinary life remains decorative, the acceptance map is not satisfied.

## H. Engineering And Civilization Doctrine

Engineering, standardization, industrialization, infrastructure, institutions, governance, science, and large-scale coordination are first-class demands.

Dominium must therefore be judgeable against whether it can lawfully support:

- invention beyond recipes
- reusable designs and blueprints
- validation and standards
- schools, factories, workshops, bureaucracies, and legal systems
- logistics and infrastructure at scale
- city and civilization continuity
- long-run revision, maintenance, and supersession of designs, procedures, and institutions

If these are treated as optional extras, the semantic framework is being underused.

## I. Scale And Continuity Doctrine

Different desire families require different mixtures of:

- local embodied detail
- macro summarized support
- lawful collapse and expansion
- historical lineage preservation
- institutional continuity
- capability preservation across scales

Believable support requires that macro and micro remain one lawful world. Large-scale summaries cannot contradict later detailed re-entry, and detailed play cannot behave as though offscreen civilization never existed.

## J. AI And Future Interface Doctrine

AI agents, operator tools, and future interfaces are alternate lawful access paths over the same world substrate.

They may differ in:

- access route
- authority envelope
- observability
- planning horizon
- mediation tooling

They may not differ in:

- ontology
- truth source
- lawful Process mutation path
- substitution rules
- lineage obligations

This means later `Σ`, `Φ`, `Υ`, and `Ζ` work must inherit the same acceptance map rather than inventing special AI-only or operator-only universes.

## K. Gap And Insufficiency Framing

This document is an acceptance constitution, not a claim that every family is fully implemented.

Current state is mixed:

- law, authority, observation, audit, AI/delegation, modding, and formalization have strong grounding
- engineering, maintenance, and machine-related demand have strong semantic grounding with selective code embodiment
- ordinary life, agriculture, full economy, urban systems, conflict, and offworld support are less fully grounded in instantiated domain constitutions
- later `Λ-5½`, `Λ-6`, `Σ`, `Φ`, `Υ`, and `Ζ` work must treat this map as a constraint and prioritization floor

No later prompt may claim success purely because a subsystem is elegant. It must demonstrate believable support or explicit lawful refusal against the relevant desire families.

## L. Anti-Patterns And Forbidden Shapes

Forbidden shapes include:

- treating desires as roadmap bullets instead of substrate-grounded acceptance criteria
- claiming "players can do X" without domain, capability, formalization, and scaling support
- reducing the project to survival/crafting shorthand
- reducing engineering to recipe slots
- reducing civilization to building count and NPC count
- reducing ordinary life to flavor dressing
- treating AI or future interfaces as a separate ontology
- treating present repo embodiment as proof of complete support
- hiding unresolved substrate gaps behind UI polish or narrative convenience

## M. Stability And Evolution

This artifact is `stable`.

Later prompts may:

- refine desire families into domain-specific acceptance tests
- add lawful future desire families
- specialize family support criteria per domain or experience profile
- bind runtime, governance, provenance, and live-ops work to these acceptance expectations

Later prompts may not:

- silently demote ordinary life
- silently demote engineering, institutions, or civilization
- treat AI/future interfaces as separate ontologies
- weaken substrate grounding requirements
- replace this map with a marketing-facing feature list
