# Domain Constitution Execution Plan

## A. Purpose And Scope

This artifact is `POST-ZETA-6`.

It defines the next actual execution order for the domain constitution program after admissible `Ζ` closure and post-`Ζ` template freezes.

It answers:

- what the next drafting prompt should be
- what order the domain constitutions should follow
- what must not start until earlier domains and bridge assumptions are frozen

## B. Recommended Next Prompt

- lawful primitive substrate

## C. Recommended Domain Constitution Order

### 1. `DOMAIN-0 — MATTER_PHYSICS_MATERIALS_CONSTRUCTION_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why first:

- establishes primitive substrate, material change, conservation, and construction grammar
- anchors later machine, transport, biology, settlement, and industrial domains

### 2. `DOMAIN-1 — GEOLOGY_HYDROLOGY_CLIMATE_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why second:

- turns terrain, water, climate, and planetary conditions into explicit substrate
- must inherit material and physical law before resource, city, agriculture, or transport constitutions

### 3. `DOMAIN-2 — TRANSPORT_INFRASTRUCTURE_NETWORKS_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why third:

- depends on materials and terrain
- becomes a major bridge host for logistics, settlement, governance, and offworld continuity

### 4. `DOMAIN-3 — BIOLOGY_ECOLOGY_MEDICINE_EVOLUTION_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why fourth:

- depends on material and environmental substrate
- provides life, health, food, and ecological continuity for ordinary-life, agriculture, and civilization work

### 5. `DOMAIN-4 — AGENTS_MINDS_LABOR_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why fifth:

- depends on biology and interface-neutral action law
- anchors labor, delegation, agency, and institutional participation

### 6. `DOMAIN-5 — KNOWLEDGE_LANGUAGE_CULTURE_INSTITUTIONS_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why sixth:

- depends on agents, formalization lineage, and observational law
- stabilizes how explicit knowledge, education, culture, and institutions propagate

### 7. `DOMAIN-6 — ECONOMY_LOGISTICS_INDUSTRY_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why seventh:

- depends on matter, transport, agents, and knowledge
- requires prior bridge and verification structure to avoid becoming abstract accounting magic

### 8. `DOMAIN-7 — POLITICS_LAW_GOVERNANCE_WAR_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why eighth:

- depends on institutions, economy, infrastructure, and agents
- must not be written before jurisdiction, logistics, and organizational substrate are explicit

### 9. `DOMAIN-8 — SPACE_ASTRONOMY_OFFWORLD_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why ninth:

- depends on transport, infrastructure, biology, governance, and material substrate
- is far easier to write honestly after terrestrial continuity and civilization scaling are explicit

### 10. `DOMAIN-9 — INTERFACE_EMBODIMENT_PERCEPTION_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why tenth:

- should adapt previously frozen domain truth rather than becoming a hidden ontology owner
- must consume earlier domains and preserve truth/perceived/render law

### 11. `DOMAIN-10 — PERSISTENCE_NETWORKING_REPLAY_MODULARITY_CONSTITUTION-0`

Why last:

- is cross-cutting and must preserve all prior domain meanings rather than define them
- should consolidate replay, persistence, networking, and modular continuity after the major domain semantics are explicit

## E. Review Gates

Human review remains required when:

- a domain constitution tries to absorb ownership from another domain silently
- a domain constitution weakens doctrine versus admission versus realization distinctions
- a domain constitution uses frontier live-ops items as if they were closed
- interface or persistence constitutions try to redefine domain truth rather than adapt it

## F. Stop Conditions

Stop the run if:

- a domain constitution lacks primitive substrate, Process, failure, verification, or scale-path sections
- a domain constitution claims bridge legality without using the bridge template
- a domain constitution claims desire support without acceptance evidence expectations
- frontier live-ops realization is smuggled back in as domain law

## G. What Not To Start Early

Do not start:

- economy/logistics/industry before material, terrain, transport, agents, and knowledge substrate are frozen
- politics/law/governance/war before institutions, economy, and infrastructure are explicit
- space/offworld before terrestrial continuity and life-support dependencies are explicit
- interface/embodiment/perception before earlier domains exist to be perceived
- persistence/networking/replay/modularity before prior domains exist to be preserved
