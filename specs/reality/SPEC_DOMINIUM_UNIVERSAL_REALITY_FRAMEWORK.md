# Dominium Universal Reality Framework v1

## A. Purpose And Scope

This document is the master semantic and reality framework for Dominium after Ω, Ξ, Π, P-0 through P-4, and `0-0`.

It governs:

- the constitutional meaning of reality in Dominium
- the boundary between Truth, Perceived state, and Rendered state
- the relationship between total truth and sparse materialization
- the lawful use of refinement, substitution, capsules, and representation ladders
- the continuity obligations that later domains, runtime systems, governance systems, and release systems must preserve

It does not yet define:

- every domain constitution
- the detailed domain contract template
- the detailed capability catalog
- the full representation ladder taxonomy
- cross-domain ownership winners for disputed repo roots
- runtime service implementations or materialization engines

This framework extends the frozen Constitutional Architecture.
It does not renegotiate it.
Later domain constitutions, capability constitutions, governance contracts, and runtime constitutions must inherit from this framework.

This framework is also repo-reality-grounded.
It is not a clean-room theory.
It formalizes the semantic direction already embodied across live roots including `worldgen`, `geo`, `reality`, `materials`, `logic`, `signals`, `system`, `universe`, `epistemics`, `diegetics`, `infrastructure`, `machines`, and pack-backed law and experience surfaces.

## B. Core Reality Doctrine

Dominium reality is the single lawful world substrate governed by Law, Process, provenance, and deterministic execution.

The three primary state classes are:

- `Truth`: authoritative world state and all authoritative commitments required to determine lawful outcomes
- `Perceived`: observer-scoped, authority-scoped, and lens-scoped projection derived from Truth plus lawful epistemic artifacts
- `Rendered`: presentation-specific projection derived from Perceived state and presentational contracts

The boundaries are hard:

- Truth MUST NOT be redefined by Perceived or Rendered state.
- Perceived state MUST NOT mutate Truth.
- Rendered state MUST NOT mutate Truth or silently decide law, authority, or ontology.
- Observation and perception MAY filter, redact, summarize, quantize, or defer access, but they MUST NOT rewrite what is true.
- Rendering MAY choose presentation forms, but it MUST NOT create authoritative facts.

Nothing in the optimization, interface, tooling, or network stack may leak across these boundaries in a way that changes meaning silently.

## C. Truth Totality

Truth in Dominium is total and authoritative.

`Total` means:

- for any lawful world question that falls within the declared world scope and the relevant interaction or observation horizon, there exists one authoritative answer or one authoritative refusal / unknown form declared by law
- authoritative truth is grounded in the lawful substrate, not in whichever representation is currently materialized
- absence of present materialization does not imply absence of truth

`Total` does not mean:

- every possible detail must be eagerly computed at all times
- every observer is entitled to access all truth
- every question is always answerable at the same fidelity

Unmaterialized truth is not undefined.
It may be latent, summarized, conditionally derivable, or represented through lawful condensed forms, but it is still part of the authoritative substrate.

Undefined is lawful only when the framework or a later domain constitution explicitly declares that a phenomenon is outside scope, absent, or not committed at that layer.
Optimization, distance, or viewer convenience are not valid reasons to convert truth into undefined state.

## D. Sparse Materialization

Dominium uses sparse materialization.

This means computation, refinement, and explicit detail realization are conditional rather than universal.

The system MAY defer:

- high-cost detail that is not yet required for the current interaction horizon
- derived observations and presentation artifacts
- fine-grained expansion of macro or substituted representations
- local detail that has no current causal, legal, or observational requirement to be resolved

The system MUST NOT defer:

- any authoritative commitments required to determine lawful outcomes at the current horizon
- any conservation, provenance, or identity facts already committed by truth
- any information required to keep replay, audit, compatibility, or deterministic causality lawful
- any state needed to decide whether an action is allowed, refused, or transformed under law

Sparse materialization is therefore a performance and representation discipline, not a semantic loophole.
It changes when and how detail is resolved, not what the world means.

## E. Causal Closure

Resolved state MUST be causally sufficient for the relevant interaction and observation horizon.

`Minimal causal closure` means the smallest authoritative resolved state that is sufficient to:

- determine all lawful outcomes that can affect the current interaction horizon
- preserve declared invariants
- support lawful observation at the current epistemic horizon
- avoid hidden dependence on unspecified or ad hoc invented facts

Minimal causal closure prevents fake worlds.
A condensed, deferred, or capsule representation is lawful only if it is sufficient to produce the same authoritative consequences for the relevant horizon as the expanded form would produce.

If a representation cannot answer the current lawful question set without guesswork, it is not causally closed enough for that horizon and MUST be expanded, refused, or escalated explicitly.

## F. Epistemic And Diegetic Refinement

Observation, knowledge, sensing, instrumentation, memory, and diegetic access determine what is materialized or resolved for perception.

Refinement MAY depend on:

- AuthorityContext
- LawProfile and ExperienceProfile
- declared epistemic artifacts such as observations, measurements, records, instruments, and memory
- declared interest-region or interaction triggers
- deterministic budget and fidelity policies

Refinement MUST NOT silently depend on:

- camera zoom alone
- renderer privilege
- out-of-band tool privilege that bypasses law
- wall-clock time
- thread timing
- ad hoc human convenience

Epistemic and diegetic refinement may change what an observer can lawfully know, inspect, or resolve.
It must not redefine objective truth.
Ignorance, uncertainty, misinformation, secrecy, and redaction are modeled as epistemic conditions, not as edits to the authoritative world substrate.

## G. Representation Doctrine

Reality may have multiple lawful representations.

A representation is lawful only if it preserves the invariants and commitments explicitly declared for its scope.
Representations may differ in:

- fidelity
- abstraction level
- access scope
- serialization or storage form
- presentational projection

Representations MUST preserve declared meaning.
They MUST NOT silently redefine identity, conservation, topology, causality, authority, or domain commitments.

Optimization MAY change representation.
Optimization MUST NOT change semantics silently.

Every representation used downstream must declare:

- which invariants it preserves directly
- which commitments it summarizes
- which conditions require expansion or escalation
- which questions it cannot answer without lawful refinement

## H. Representation Ladders (High-Level)

At the universal framework level, Dominium recognizes the following ladder families:

- `primitive`: low-level substrate commitments and direct truth-bearing state
- `recognized`: structured recognition of patterns, entities, or relations grounded in primitive substrate
- `formalized`: explicit domain concepts with declared contracts and invariants
- `substituted`: lawful capsule, summary, proxy, or condensed form standing in for a more detailed representation
- `macro/micro`: scale-linked representations with different granularity over the same truth substrate

These ladders are not separate universes.
They are lawful views and commitments over the same reality.

Later Λ prompts refine the detailed ladder rules.
This document freezes the universal requirement that ladders must remain semantically aligned, traceable, and non-silent in their meaning changes.

## I. Semantic Ascent And Descent (High-Level)

Semantic ascent is the lawful emergence of higher-order meaning from lower-order substrate.
Semantic descent is the lawful recovery, expansion, or projection of higher-order commitments back into operationally relevant detail.

At the framework level:

- ascent MUST be grounded in lawful substrate, provenance, and declared recognition or formalization rules
- descent MUST preserve continuity with the higher-order commitments already made
- ascent and descent MUST remain auditable rather than magical

Higher-order representations may be more useful, more compact, or more legible.
They do not gain permission to contradict the substrate they summarize.

When descent cannot recover detail uniquely, the system MUST:

- expand from authoritative substrate
- preserve explicit uncertainty
- or refuse to overclaim

It MUST NOT invent hidden detail to make a higher-order representation look complete.

## J. Substitution And Capsule Doctrine

Substitution is lawful when one representation stands in for another without violating declared invariants, continuity anchors, or causal closure requirements.

Capsules are lawful when they are:

- grounded in authoritative truth
- bound to declared invariants and conserved commitments
- accompanied by explicit activation, expansion, and refusal conditions
- sufficient for the current causal horizon

Capsules are not fake.
They are canonical condensed forms of reality when used under declared constraints.

Substitution is invalid if it:

- changes meaning silently
- loses required provenance or identity anchors
- breaks conservation or topology commitments
- creates or removes lawful affordances without explicit rule
- causes observer-visible discontinuity that is not explained by world law or lawful perception change

Later Λ prompts must define more detailed proof and equivalence criteria, but this framework freezes the rule that substitution and collapse are lawful only when invariants are preserved and the relevant horizon remains causally closed.

## K. Experiential Continuity Doctrine

Optimization and representation change MUST preserve player-observable continuity.

`Experiential continuity` means:

- the world does not appear to teleport, rewrite itself, or contradict prior lawful observation without cause
- identity anchors remain stable across expansion, collapse, substitution, migration, and projection changes
- observable cause and effect remain consistent with world law
- changes in fidelity or access are explainable by lawful refinement, observation, travel, distance, instrumentation, authority, or process outcomes

Experiential continuity does not require identical pixels or identical data layouts.
It requires stable meaning from the perspective of lawful experience.

Expansion, collapse, or substitution that produces arbitrary world jumps, unexplained contradictions, or hidden retcon behavior is forbidden.

## L. Macro ↔ Micro Consistency

Macro and micro are lawful granularity layers over the same truth substrate.

The macro form MAY summarize.
The micro form MAY expand.
Neither may silently contradict the other.

Macro-to-micro expansion MUST preserve:

- declared conserved quantities
- topology and spatial commitments already made
- identity and provenance anchors
- law-gated capabilities and constraints already committed

Micro-to-macro collapse MUST preserve:

- authoritative commitments that remain relevant after collapse
- enough summary state to keep future expansion and interaction lawful
- enough invariants to prevent semantic drift or continuity loss

Macro and micro consistency matters because later civilization, logistics, world, and materialization systems depend on movement between condensed and detailed forms without ontology replacement.

## M. Interface-Agnostic Reality

The truth substrate is interface-agnostic.

The same world must support:

- embodied players
- abstract planners
- AI agents
- NPC labor and institutional actors
- future interfaces not yet defined

Interface changes may alter:

- access path
- lawful perception
- presentation shape
- action affordances granted by profile or authority

They MUST NOT alter what reality is.

No domain or runtime subsystem may bind core world semantics to one presentation mode, one client form, or one agent style.
Capability surfaces will later formalize interface-specific access without allowing interface-specific ontology.

## N. Domain Inheritance Rule

All later domain constitutions MUST inherit from this framework.

No domain may locally redefine:

- the meaning of Truth, Perceived, or Rendered state
- truth totality
- sparse materialization
- causal closure
- lawful representation and substitution
- experiential continuity
- macro/micro consistency
- interface-agnostic reality

Domains MAY extend this framework by defining:

- domain-specific conserved quantities
- domain-specific refinement triggers
- domain-specific formalization chains
- domain-specific capability contracts
- domain-specific lawful refusal conditions

Those extensions must strengthen and specialize this framework.
They must not silently override it.

## O. Runtime And Governance Implications

Later `Σ` may now assume:

- governance, task, safety, and wrapper systems must preserve the truth / perceived / render boundary
- natural-language and agent interfaces may request, inspect, or plan, but they do not redefine reality
- refusal, redaction, and capability gating are lawful semantic outcomes, not implementation defects

Later `Φ` may now assume:

- sparse materialization is allowed only through lawful representation and substitution
- runtime optimization must preserve semantic invariants and causal closure
- expansion, collapse, event logging, snapshotting, and state externalization must preserve identity, provenance, and continuity
- runtime services must treat unmaterialized truth as latent authoritative substrate, not as undefined space to improvise into

Later `Υ` and `Ζ` must not:

- treat deployment, release, migration, rollback, or live operations as permission to alter semantic meaning silently
- collapse compatibility identity into informal naming or presentation labels
- authorize runtime substitution, coexistence, migration, or cutover behavior that violates continuity or invariant preservation

## Q. Stability And Evolution Notes

Stability class: `stable`.

This artifact is stable enough for later Λ, Σ, and Φ prompts to cite directly.
Later prompts may refine, extend, or operationalize this framework.
They MUST NOT silently override it.

Any future change that alters the meaning of Truth, materialization, representation, substitution, continuity, or inheritance requires:

- explicit artifact update
- explicit versioning
- explicit semantic contract pinning
- explicit downstream impact review

Compatibility and semantic identity must remain explicit.
Later prompts may add domain detail, ladder detail, proof criteria, and operational contracts, but the universal framework frozen here remains the floor they inherit from.
