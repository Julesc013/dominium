# DOMINIUM TRADEMARK AND PROJECT IDENTITY POLICY
## Version 1.0

This policy governs use of Dominium and Domino project identity.

`LICENSE.md` governs use of the repository contents. This file governs names, marks, public identity, affiliation, and endorsement.

---

# 1. RESERVED IDENTITY

The Project Owner reserves all rights in the following names and identity surfaces:

- Dominium
- Domino, when used as the name of the Dominium deterministic substrate or related project technology
- Dominium Engine
- Dominium Game
- Dominium Workbench
- associated logos, icons, marks, names, slogans, badges, account names, domain names, visual identity, repository identity, and trade dress
- confusingly similar names, marks, or presentation that could imply official status, affiliation, endorsement, sponsorship, or continuity with Dominium

This policy applies whether or not a mark is registered.

---

# 2. PERMITTED NOMINATIVE USE

You may use the reserved names in plain text where necessary to truthfully identify or discuss the official project, such as:

- linking to the Official Repository;
- saying that a bug report, security report, article, review, compatibility note, or discussion concerns Dominium;
- describing a contribution submitted to the Official Repository;
- making non-commercial commentary, criticism, research, or educational discussion about the project.

Permitted nominative use must be accurate, non-misleading, and no more prominent than necessary.

---

# 3. PROHIBITED USE

Unless the Project Owner gives prior express written permission, You may not use reserved project identity to:

1. name, brand, promote, or distribute a fork, clone, modified version, derivative work, server, service, launcher, pack, marketplace, mod loader, SDK, toolchain, product, platform, or commercial offering;
2. imply that Your work is official, endorsed, sponsored, approved, compatible, certified, maintained, or affiliated with Dominium;
3. register or operate domains, package names, social accounts, app names, store listings, repository names, organisation names, product names, or community hubs that use or are confusingly similar to reserved project identity;
4. use Dominium or Domino logos, icons, trade dress, badges, screenshots, marks, or visual identity as the primary branding for Your own work;
5. remove, alter, obscure, or replace official attribution, copyright, license, provenance, audit, diagnostic, or ownership notices;
6. create public confusion about the source, ownership, authority, compatibility, release status, security status, or governance status of any material.

---

# 4. NAMING FOR UNOFFICIAL MATERIAL

If the Project Owner permits an unofficial integration, guide, report, article, or community resource, use naming that makes unofficial status clear.

Acceptable patterns include:

- `Unofficial guide for Dominium`
- `Compatibility notes for Dominium`
- `Security report concerning Dominium`
- `Personal study notes about Dominium`

Do not use patterns that imply official status, such as:

- `Dominium Pro`
- `Dominium Community Edition`
- `Dominium Server`
- `Dominium SDK`
- `Dominium Launcher`
- `Official Dominium Mod Hub`
- `Dominium-compatible`, unless the Project Owner has expressly granted compatibility certification

---

# 5. CONTRIBUTIONS AND ACCEPTED MATERIAL

Submitting a Contribution does not grant You any trademark, branding, endorsement, naming, distribution, commercialisation, or independent publication right.

Accepted Contributions become governed Project material under `CONTRIBUTOR_LICENSE_AGREEMENT.md` and may be used by the Project Owner under official Dominium identity.

---

# 6. FAN AND COMMUNITY CONTENT

Fan, commentary, educational, and community content may be separately addressed by `FAN_CONTENT_POLICY.md` where that file exists.

If a fan or community use conflicts with this policy, this policy controls unless the Project Owner gives prior express written permission.

---

# 7. ENFORCEMENT

The Project Owner may require correction, takedown, renaming, disclaimer changes, or cessation of use for any identity use that creates confusion, violates this policy, violates `LICENSE.md`, violates `CONTRIBUTOR_LICENSE_AGREEMENT.md`, or harms the project.

Failure to enforce this policy in one case does not waive the Project Owner's right to enforce it in another case.

---

# 8. CONTACT

Trademark, naming, compatibility, endorsement, domain, account, logo, or public identity permission requests should be directed through the Official Repository or another contact channel formally published by the Project Owner.
